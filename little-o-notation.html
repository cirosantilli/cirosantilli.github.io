Little-o notation (o(n))

| 🗖 nosplit | ↑ parent "Big O notation" | words: 69
🗖 nosplit | ⇓ toc | ↑ parent "Big O notation" | words: 69
🔗
Stronger version of the big O notation, basically means that ratio goes to zero. In Big O, the ratio does not need to go to zero.
🔗
So in informal terms, big O notation means ≤, and little-o notation means <.
🔗
E.g. x=O(x) but x​=o(x) and x=o(2x) but

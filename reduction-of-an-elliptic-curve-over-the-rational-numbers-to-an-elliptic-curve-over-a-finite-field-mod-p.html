Reduction of an elliptic curve over the rational numbers to an elliptic curve over a finite field mod p (Reduction of an elliptic curve from E(Q) to E(Fp​)modp)

OurBigBook.com Words: 106
This construction takes as input:
  • elliptic curve over the rational numbers
  • a prime number p
and it produces an elliptic curve over a finite field of order p as output.
The constructions is used in the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture.
To do it, we just convert the coefficients a and b from the Equation 1. "Definition of the elliptic curves" from rational numbers to elements of the finite field.
For example, suppose we have a=3/4 and we are using p=11.
For the denominator 4, we just use the multiplicative inverse, e.g. supposing we have
43​→3×4−1mod11=3×3mod11=9mod11
(9)
where 4−1=3mod11 because 4×3=1mod11, related: math.stackexchange.com/questions/1204034/elliptic-curve-reduction-modulo-p

OK, so let's verify the main desired consequence of the Lorentz transformation: that everyone observes the same speed of light.
🔗
Observers will measure the speed of light by calculating how long it takes the light going towards +x cross a rod of length L=x2​−x1​ laid in the x axis at position X1.

Each observer will observe two events:
  • (t1​,x1​,y1​,z1​): the light touches the left side of the rod
  • (t2​,x2​,y2​,z2​): the light touches the right side of the rod
🔗
Supposing that the standing observer measures the speed of light as c and that light hits the left side of the rod at time T1, then he observes the coordinates:
t1​x1​t2​x2​​=T1=X1=cL​=X1+L​

🔗
Now, if we transform for the moving observer:
t1′​x1′​t2′​x2′​​=γ(t1​−c2vx1​​)=γ(x1​−vt1​)=γ(t2​−c2vx2​​)=γ(x2​−vt2​)​
(12)
and so the moving observer measures the speed of light as:
c′​=t2′​−t1′​x2′​−x1′​​=(t2​−c2vx2​​)−(t1​−c2vx1​​)(x2​−vt2​)−(x1​−vt1​)​=(t2​−t1​)−c2v​(x2​−x1​)(x2​−x1​)−v(t2​−t1​)​=1−c2v​t2​−t1​x2​−x1​​t2​−t1​x2​−x1​​−v​=1−c2v​cc−v​=cc−v​c−v​=c​

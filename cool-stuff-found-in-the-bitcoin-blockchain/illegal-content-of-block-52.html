blk00052.txt contains the first noticeable attempts at insertion of possibly illegal content into the blockchain:
  • bitcoin data download and upload scripts from blk0052.dat
  • tx 08654f9dc9d673b3527b48ad06ab1b199ad47b61fd54033af30c2ee975c588bd contains a leaked private key and a link to: http://threatpost.com/en_us/blogs/ami-firmware-source-codAe-private-key-leaked-040513
  • tx 54e48e5f5c656b26c3bca14a8c95aa583d07ebe84dde3b7dd4a78f4e4186e713 PDF file, https://bitcoin.stackexchange.com/questions/35959/how-is-the-whitepaper-decoded-from-the-blockchain-tx-with-1000x-m-of-n-multisi suggests it is the Bitcoin white paper
  • tx dde7cd8e8f073a525c16c5ee4e4a254f847b7ad6babef257231813166fbef551 and tx 4a0088a249e9099d205fb4760c28275d4b8965ac9fd56f5ddf6771cdb0d94f38 contain indexes of pages from The Hidden Wiki
  • tx b96af3b69b48a82c5eae3e44ebb6ef93f30d7764b1d5b40243e11b0d374ac1b7 http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illegal_prime followed presumably by one such prime
  • tx 691dd277dc0e90a462a3d652a1171686de49cf19067cd33c7df0392833fb986a Wikileaks Cablegate Backup. Mentioned in Hidden surprises in the Bitcoin blockchain by Ken Shirriff (2014)

Quantum electrodynamics Lagrangian (QED Lagrangian)

 ... Physics Particle physics Quantum mechanics Relativistic quantum mechanics Quantum field theory Quantum electrodynamics
OurBigBook.com Words: 326 Articles: 1
LQED​=ψˉ​(iℏcD/ −mc2)ψ−4μ0​1​Fμν​Fμν
(1)
where:
  • F is the electromagnetic tensor
Note that this is the sum of the:
  • Dirac Lagrangian, which only describes the "inertia of bodies" part of the equation
  • the electromagnetic interaction term 4μ0​1​Fμν​Fμν, which describes term describes forces
Note that the relationship between ψ and F is not explicit. However, if we knew what type of particle we were talking about, e.g. electron, then the knowledge of psi would also give the charge distribution and therefore F
As mentioned at the beginning of Quantum Field Theory lecture notes by David Tong (2007):
  • by "Lagrangian" we mean Lagrangian density
  • the generalized coordinates of the Lagrangian are fields

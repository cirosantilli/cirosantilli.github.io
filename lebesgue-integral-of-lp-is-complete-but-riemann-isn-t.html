Lebesgue integral of Lp is complete but Riemann isn't

ï€• Home Mathematics Area of mathematics Calculus Integral Lebesgue integral
OurBigBook.com Words: 490 Articles: 11
Lp is:
  • complete under the Lebesgue integral, this result is may be called the Riesz-Fischer theorem
  • not complete under the Riemann integral: math.stackexchange.com/questions/397369/space-of-riemann-integrable-functions-not-complete
And then this is why quantum mechanics basically lives in L2: not being complete makes no sense physically, it would mean that you can get closer and closer to states that don't exist!
